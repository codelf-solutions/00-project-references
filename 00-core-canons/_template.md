## Structural Patterns

**Paragraph Length:** [short 2-3 sentences | medium 4-6 sentences | long 7+ sentences | mixed]
- Short: Quick pacing, emphasizes key points
- Medium: Balanced detail and readability
- Long: Complex arguments, multiple connected thoughts
- Mixed: Varied for rhythm and engagement

**List Frequency:** [rare | moderate | frequent]
- Rare: Prefer prose (academic, legal, narrative)
- Moderate: Lists used strategically when beneficial
- Frequent: Lists default for clarity and scannability (technical specs, procedures)

**Active vs Passive Voice:** [percentage active, e.g., 80% active | balanced | field-appropriate]
- Active: "I conducted tests" (more human, engaging)
- Passive: "Tests were conducted" (formal, objective)
- Field-appropriate: Scientific papers typically use passive voice as discipline standard

**Contractions:** [always | context-dependent | never]
- Always: "I'm," "it's," "don't" used naturally throughout
- Context-dependent: Used in informal (memos, guides, emails) but avoided in formal (contracts, proposals)
- Never: Full forms only ("I am," "it is," "do not")

**Fourth-Wall Breaking:** [never breaks | occasionally for emphasis | not applicable]
- Never breaks: Does not reference the document itself ("This document will...") 
- Occasionally: Rare, used strategically for emphasis
- Not applicable: Document type doesn't have fourth-wall concern

## Authority & Certainty

**Claims Backed By:** [data always | citations when possible | experience/judgment | varies]
- Data always: Every claim must be quantified (percentages, timelines, measurements)
- Citations when possible: Prefer attributed sources, but judgment acceptable
- Experience/judgment: Personal expertise carries weight, doesn't require external validation
- Varies: Depends on claim type and audience expectations

**Uncertainty Expression:** [explicit "unknown" | qualified "likely" | avoided]
- Explicit "unknown": States clearly "This is unknown" or "We don't have data on this"
- Qualified "likely": Uses measured hedging only when genuinely uncertain
- Avoided: Presents information as settled unless genuine uncertainty exists

**Confidence Level:** [humble | balanced | authoritative]
- Humble: Acknowledges limitations, invites collaboration, opens to challenge
- Balanced: Confident in expertise but acknowledges complexity
- Authoritative: Speaks with certainty, positions self as expert, limited qualification
